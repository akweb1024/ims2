11. PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT SYSTEM (PMS)
This Performance Management System (PMS) policy defines ITBREAKCOM Private Limited’s structured, data-driven approach to managing performance. It establishes quarterly KPIs, monthly reviews, dashboards, and scorecards, with a direct linkage between performance outcomes, compensation, promotion, development actions, and exits. This policy forms an integral part of the Board-approved HR Governance Pack.
11.1 OBJECTIVE
The objectives of the PMS are to:• Align individual performance with organizational goals and revenue targets• Establish measurable, outcome-based KPIs• Enable continuous feedback and course correction• Drive a high-performance culture• Ensure fairness and transparency in pay, promotion, and exit decisions
11.2 SCOPE & APPLICABILITY
This PMS applies to:• All employees (M1–M6)• Trainees transitioning to employee rolesConsultants are governed by contractual performance clauses.
11.3 PERFORMANCE CYCLE
The standard performance cycle includes:• Quarterly KPI setting• Monthly performance reviews• Mid-cycle corrections (if required)• Annual performance summary
11.4 KPI & KRA FRAMEWORK
• KPIs and KRAs shall be role-specific and aligned with business priorities.• KPIs may include revenue, delivery, quality, efficiency, and behavior metrics.• Each KPI shall have defined targets and weightages.• KPI scorecards shall be documented and acknowledged.
11.5 MONTHLY REVIEWS & FEEDBACK
• Managers shall conduct monthly one-on-one reviews.• Progress against KPIs shall be discussed.• Feedback shall be documented and shared.• Early identification of performance risks is encouraged.

• Performance dashboards may be maintained through HRMS / CRM tools.• Dashboards shall provide visibility into individual, team, and function-level performance.• Data integrity and confidentiality shall be maintained.

9. COMPENSATION PHILOSOPHY & CTC DESIGN
This Compensation Philosophy & CTC Design policy defines ITBREAK COM Private Limited’s approach to employee compensation. It establishes a pay-for-performance model with a minimum CTC threshold, a structured fixed and variable pay mix, and compliance optimization for PF, ESIC, and investor scrutiny. This policy forms an integral part of the Board-approved HR Governance Pack.
9.1 OBJECTIVE
The objectives of this policy are to:• Attract and retain high-quality talent• Align compensation with performance and business outcomes• Ensure internal equity and market competitiveness• Maintain statutory compliance (PF, ESIC, Bonus)• Meet investor expectations on compensation governance
9.2 COMPENSATION PHILOSOPHY
• The Company follows a pay-for-performance philosophy.• Compensation decisions are based on role value, M-Level, performance, and market benchmarks.• Fixed pay ensures income stability; variable pay rewards outcomes.• Compensation structures are designed for scalability and transparency.
9.3 MINIMUM CTC THRESHOLD
• The minimum Cost to Company (CTC) for any full-time employee shall be ₹23,000 per month.• This threshold supports statutory wage requirements and fair compensation practices.• Any exception requires Managing Director and HR approval.
9.4 CTC STRUCTURE – FIXED & VARIABLE
CTC shall typically comprise:Fixed Components:• Basic Salary• House Rent Allowance (HRA), where applicable• Other allowancesVariable Components:• Performance-linked incentives• Sales or revenue incentives• Annual / quarterly bonuses
9.5 STATUTORY OPTIMIZATION (PF / ESIC)
• PF contributions shall be calculated on Basic Salary as per law.• ESIC applicability shall be determined based on statutory wage thresholds.• CTC structures shall be designed to ensure compliance without artificial wage splitting.• Any changes in statutory limits shall be incorporated promptly.
